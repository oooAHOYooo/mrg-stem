Unreal Engine 5 - Level Design

Create your own 4-season world you can walk through!

üéÆ Start Here for Unreal Engine!

Follow these steps to download Unreal Engine and start building your world!

üì• Step 1: Download Unreal Engine at Home

1 Go to the Unreal Engine Website

Visit www.unrealengine.com

Click the big "Download Now" button at the top of the page.

2 Create a Free Account

You'll need to make an account (it's free!). Ask a parent or guardian to help you:

  • Click "Sign Up" or "Create Account"
  • Enter your email address (or your parent's email)
  • Create a password (write it down somewhere safe!)
  • Check your email and click the link to confirm your account

3 Download Epic Games Launcher

After you sign in, you'll download the Epic Games Launcher:

  • Click "Download" for the Epic Games Launcher
  • Run the installer (ask a parent for help if needed)
  • Sign in to the launcher with your account

4 Install Unreal Engine

In the Epic Games Launcher:

  • Click on "Unreal Engine" in the left menu
  • Click "Install Engine"
  • Choose the latest version (like 5.3 or 5.4)
  • Click "Install" - this will take a while (maybe 30-60 minutes)
  • Go get a snack while it downloads! üçé

Important: Unreal Engine is BIG (about 10-15 GB), so make sure you have enough space on your computer!

üí° Tips for Parents

  • Unreal Engine is completely FREE for students and learning
  • It works on Windows and Mac computers
  • Make sure your computer has enough space (at least 20 GB free)
  • The download might take 30-60 minutes depending on your internet
  • If you have any problems, check the Unreal Engine website for help

üé® Step 2: Getting Started with Free Assets

Unreal Engine comes with lots of FREE stuff you can use to build your world!

1 Open Unreal Engine

After Unreal Engine finishes installing:

  • Open the Epic Games Launcher
  • Click "Launch" next to Unreal Engine
  • When it opens, click "New Project"
  • Choose "Games" ‚Üí "Third Person" template
  • Give your project a name like "My4SeasonWorld"
  • Click "Create"

2 Get Free Assets from the Marketplace

Unreal Engine has a FREE marketplace with tons of stuff:

  • In Unreal Engine, click "Edit" ‚Üí "Plugins"
  • Or go to unrealengine.com/marketplace
  • Look for things marked "FREE" or "Free for the Month"
  • Click on something you like, then click "Add to Project"

3 Great Free Assets to Start With

Here are some FREE things perfect for making a 4-season world:

  • Starter Content - Already in your project! Trees, rocks, and more
  • Landscape Materials - Search for "grass", "snow", "autumn leaves"
  • Nature Packs - Look for "free nature" or "free trees"
  • Weather Effects - Search for "snow", "rain", "sun" effects

Remember: Always look for the "FREE" label before adding anything!

üåç Step 3: Create Your 4-Season World

Now let's build a world with four different seasons you can walk through!

1 Set Up Your World

When your project opens, you'll see a character in a gray world:

  • Press F to focus on your character
  • You can click and drag to look around
  • Use the mouse wheel to zoom in and out

2 Make Four Different Areas (One for Each Season)

We'll create four sections: Spring, Summer, Fall, and Winter!

üå± Spring Area:

  • In the Content Browser (bottom), search for "grass" or "flowers"
  • Drag green grass and colorful flowers into your world
  • Add some small trees with green leaves
  • Place this in the front-left area of your world

‚òÄÔ∏è Summer Area:

  • Add bright green grass and big trees
  • Maybe add a sun or bright lighting
  • Place this in the front-right area

üçÇ Fall Area:

  • Search for "autumn" or "fall" materials
  • Add trees with orange, red, and yellow leaves
  • Add some fallen leaves on the ground
  • Place this in the back-left area

‚ùÑÔ∏è Winter Area:

  • Search for "snow" materials
  • Add white snow on the ground
  • Add trees without leaves (bare branches)
  • Maybe add some snowflakes or ice
  • Place this in the back-right area

3 Use Third Person Perspective

The Third Person template means you'll see your character from behind as you walk around!

  • Your project already has third person set up - that's why we chose that template!
  • To test it, click the "Play" button (green triangle) at the top
  • Use W A S D keys to move
  • Use your mouse to look around
  • Press Space to jump!
  • Press Esc to stop playing

Tip: Make sure you're in third person view (seeing your character from behind) when you play!

4 Connect Your Seasons

Make paths between your four season areas so you can walk from one to another:

  • Add paths or roads between the areas
  • You can use the landscape tool to make smooth ground
  • Add signs or markers to show which season you're entering
  • Test it by playing and walking from Spring ‚Üí Summer ‚Üí Fall ‚Üí Winter!

üéØ Your Goal

Create ONE world with FOUR different seasons that you can walk through using third person view!

  • ‚úÖ Spring area with flowers and green grass
  • ‚úÖ Summer area with bright trees and sunshine
  • ‚úÖ Fall area with colorful autumn leaves
  • ‚úÖ Winter area with snow and bare trees
  • ‚úÖ Paths connecting all four areas
  • ‚úÖ Third person camera so you see your character walking

üí° Helpful Tips

  • Save often! Press Ctrl + S (or Cmd + S on Mac) to save your work
  • Experiment! Try dragging different things into your world - you can always delete them
  • Use the search bar in the Content Browser to find things quickly
  • Ask for help if you get stuck - Mr. Gonzalez is here to help!
  • Have fun! There's no wrong way to make your world - be creative!
