## ğŸŒ‰ Bridge Time

### Goal: Build bridges with bricks that connect between tables

**Setup:**
- Students work in small groups (2-3 students per table)
- Each table builds a small city section using bricks
- Challenge: Create bridges that connect your city to neighboring tables

**Timer: Set for 25-30 minutes**

**Tips for Building Strong Bridges:**
- **Foundation First:** Make sure your bridge has a solid base on both tables
- **Support Structures:** Use columns or arches to hold up the middle of long bridges
- **Balance:** Distribute weight evenly across the bridge
- **Test as You Build:** Gently test your bridge's stability as you add pieces
- **Work Together:** Coordinate with neighboring tables to connect your bridges
- **Types to Try:** Suspension (hanging), arch (curved), beam (straight), or truss (triangular supports)
- **Start Simple:** Build a basic bridge first, then add details and decorations

**Materials Needed:**
- Building bricks/blocks (LEGO, Mega Bloks, or similar)
- Small objects to test bridge stability (optional)

**Computer Setup:**
- Ensure computers are available for students who want this option
- Provide access to Scratch (scratch.mit.edu) or Tinkercad (tinkercad.com)
- Students can work individually or in pairs on computers
